2020-Present

In my current research work I investigate the effects of interacting genetic variations on diseases using the UK-Biobank. To that end I am leveraging a framework backed-up with theoretical guarantees called Targeted Learning, which is rooted in causal inference and non-parametric statistics. The current preprint version of our work is available on the bioRxiv and the following two projects represent the main software components of the method currently under development:
  • TarGene: A Nextflow pipeline for the estimation of causal effects in population genetics via Targeted Learning.
  • TMLE.jl: A Julia package for Targeted Maximum Likelihood Estimation.
