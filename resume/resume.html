My current research work is focused on investigating the effects of interacting genetic variations on human traits using the statistical framework of Targeted Learning. Targeted Learning is a non-parametric statistical framework that enables asymptotic unbiased and efficient estimation. It is thus particularly suited to the estimation of causal quantities from large biobanks such as the UK-Biobank. The followings represent the current outcomes of my PhD project:
  • Dispensing with unnecessary assumptions in population genetics analysis: Our preprint.
  • TarGene: A Nextflow pipeline for the estimation of causal effects in population genetics via Targeted Learning.
  • TMLE.jl: A general purpose Julia package for Targeted Maximum Likelihood Estimation.
